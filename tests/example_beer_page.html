look: 4.5 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.75 | overall: 4.75

Cloudy orange-gold body topped by a clinging finger of head.

Nose is loaded with bright Nelson fruitiness - gooseberry jam and pineapple, mango, a touch of crackery malt. Subdued dank, slightly grassy. A total Nelson bomb despite the inclusion of a few other hops.

Possibly tastes even better than it smells. Juicy ripe pineapple and mango up front that suggests sweetness, which transitions into dry grassy bitter dankness. Minimal actual malt flavor. Feels a bit higher IBU than most Cellarmaker beers, though it still leans heavily toward flavor and aroma hop additions. Nice clean fermentation as usual. Possibly their finest DIPA to date, stupidly crushable yet loaded with complex hop flavors.

Serving type: growler

look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.25

A: 4.25 Hazy orange gold with a small off-white head.

S: 4.5 The aroma on this beer is wonderful. Lots of orange, mango, and even a bit of vanilla. Dank and some pine follow closely after the sweeter aromas.

T: 4.25 The taste is fairly sweet with similar characteristics as the nose. Very juicy and citrusy at first with a good amount of dank and bitterness immediately after the sweetness. It’s almost a little bit too sweet, but the lingering bitterness seems to balance it out.

M: 4.5 The beer is fairly viscous and coats the palate as you would expect from a DIPA, but still rather refreshing a drinkable. There is also a slightly dry finish that goes well with the bitterness from the hops. Overall, the mouthfeel is great, but could use a touch more carbonation.

O: 4.25 Excellent DIPA with a lot of pleasing flavors and aromas. It’s also very nice that Cellarmaker produced a beer that highlights Nelson hops, however this beer may not be quite as good as some of their other big IPAs. Still, Cellarmaker is producing fantastic beers and this is no exception.

Serving type: growler
